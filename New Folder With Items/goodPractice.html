Lets apply this

Being a minimalist I found it would be essential for me to find current examples that were simple but straight to the point and as you can see from the above examples all three designs are very basic and play a role in my design and indeed my final product.

All three designs are clean and have a lot of whitespace which as a user makes the website very easy to navigate and I don’t feel suffocated by content. In terms of user experience all the examples all make the user feel relaxed and intrigued by the what they are viewing. From a developer’s perspective, having a clean layout makes the web page very quick and slick. The use of strong background colours or images fill the page and look outstanding on modern displays. I tried to keep my designs uncluttered, by using a maximum of a three-column layout and a container with the max-width of 1170px.

Another feature they all adopt is a simple and minimal navigation something as you can see is applied in my design. From Google’s Material Design and from general research it was often the case that web pages didn’t contain the conventional navigation bars. Instead the menus were hidden and were accessed through a button and in the case of the design for Luc Hohler, Bert and then my own design a ‘burger’ icon was used. The kept the pages clean responsive and allowed more content space.

The typography on all three pages was a personal favourite of mine. I’d liked the bold and large type of Bert and Luc Hohler using them as the inspiration behind the hero text you can find on all my pages. I particularly liked the full screen hero you can find on all three and by using the 100vh CSS snippet I was able to achieve the same effect.

After completing my initial research, I then proceeded to create basic wireframes sketches these enabled me to see the basic overview of my design and then develop them into more concise mock-ups using Photoshop. On Photoshop, I like to design from mobile up. Due to the basic design, I found it was suitable to have 2 different breakpoints; one for mobile and one for larger devices. On any device smaller than 768 pixels the web page would become a one column full width page; if the device was larger than 768, it would make use of the column layout I developed.

Minimalism is at the heart of my research and is becoming increasingly popular with modern webpages. I also found that columns layouts are becoming more and more popular especially with the continuous development of frameworks like Foundation and Bootstrap. By designing my own column layout and basic container I was able to quickly develop responsive layouts.
